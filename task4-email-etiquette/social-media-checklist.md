# Social Media Do’s and Don’ts for College Students:

## Do’s ✅

1. Maintain a professional profile. Make sure your name and profile picture are appropriate, especially on platforms like LinkedIn, as recruiters often review them.

2. Be mindful before posting. Consider whether your content would be acceptable to professors or future employers.

3. Secure your accounts. Use strong, unique passwords and turn on two-factor authentication for better protection.

4. Acknowledge original creators. Whenever you share someone else’s work, give proper credit by tagging or mentioning them.

5. Limit personal information. Regularly review privacy settings and avoid making sensitive details publicly visible.

6. Take action against harmful content. Report any cyberbullying, fake profiles, or suspicious links you encounter.

## Don’ts ❌

1. Avoid sharing your real-time location. Publicly posting your current whereabouts can put your safety at risk.

2. Don’t share personal details with strangers. Unknown individuals online may have harmful intentions.

3. Avoid using social media during academic time. Posting during classes or exams can reflect poorly on your discipline.

4. Don’t spread unverified information. Always check the authenticity of news before sharing it.

5. Avoid posting negative remarks about college or individuals. Such content can damage your reputation and create long-term issues.
