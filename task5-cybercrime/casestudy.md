# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## The Incident:
In January 2025, a 45-year-old man named Rajesh Kumar from Pune got a phone call from fraud people. They told him that his bank account was involved in suspicious transactions and could be blocked anytime. They said they are from the RBI fraud department and made him panic. Then they asked him to install a screen-sharing app and stay connected on a video call. During this time, they kept guiding and pressuring him. Because of fear, he shared his banking details and approved multiple UPI payments. In total, he lost around ₹12 lakhs through different transactions. Later, he reported the case to the Cyber Crime Portal and police started investigation.

## How It Happened:
1. Fraud people called and said his bank account is linked with illegal transactions.

2. They pretended to be RBI officials to create fear and urgency.

3. They asked him to install a screen-sharing app to verify his account.

4. They watched his screen and guided him step by step.

5. They convinced him to approve multiple UPI payments.

6. When he realized it was scam, the money was already gone.

## Who Is Usually Targeted:
Working professionals, people who are not fully aware about digital scams, and those who panic quickly when they hear about bank issues or legal problems.

## Consequences:
The victim lost ₹12 lakhs. Along with financial loss, he also faced stress and anxiety. This case shows how scammers use fake authority, panic, and technology to trick people into sending money.
